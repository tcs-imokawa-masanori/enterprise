# EA Maturity Assessment Survey Questions

## Overview
This document contains the 10 detailed questions for the Enterprise Architecture Maturity Assessment. Each question measures a critical dimension of EA maturity with three progressive levels.

---

## Scoring Guide

| Level | Description | Score |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Level 0 (Initial)** | Ad-hoc, reactive, no formal processes | 0 points |
| **Level 3 (Developing)** | Basic processes established, inconsistent execution | 3 points |
| **Level 5 (Optimized)** | Mature, standardized, continuously improving | 5 points |

---

## Survey Questions

### Question 1: Architecture Documentation
**Category:** Architecture Documentation

**Question:** To what extent is your Enterprise Architecture documented and accessible?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** No formal EA documentation exists - Architecture knowledge is tribal and undocumented
- **Level 3:** Basic documentation exists but is outdated or incomplete - Some architecture artifacts available in various formats
- **Level 5:** Comprehensive EA documentation is maintained - Current state architecture is well-documented, regularly updated, and easily accessible to stakeholders

**What This Measures:**
- Documentation completeness and currency
- Accessibility of architecture information
- Knowledge management practices
- Artifact standardization

---

### Question 2: EA Governance
**Category:** EA Governance

**Question:** How mature is your Enterprise Architecture governance framework?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** No formal EA governance - Architecture decisions are made ad-hoc without oversight or standards
- **Level 3:** Basic governance structure exists - Some architecture review processes in place but inconsistently applied
- **Level 5:** Mature EA governance framework - Formal Architecture Review Board, clear decision-making processes, and compliance tracking mechanisms

**What This Measures:**
- Governance structure and formality
- Decision-making processes
- Compliance and oversight mechanisms
- Architecture Review Board effectiveness

---

### Question 3: Business Alignment
**Category:** Business Alignment

**Question:** How well does your EA align with business strategy and objectives?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** No alignment - IT and business operate independently with minimal strategic coordination
- **Level 3:** Partial alignment - Business strategy is considered but EA initiatives often lag or misalign with business priorities
- **Level 5:** Strong alignment - EA directly supports business strategy with clear traceability from business goals to architecture decisions and IT investments

**What This Measures:**
- IT-Business strategic alignment
- Traceability from business goals to IT
- Value delivery and prioritization
- Stakeholder engagement

---

### Question 4: Architecture Standards
**Category:** Architecture Standards

**Question:** Are architecture principles and standards defined and enforced across the organization?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** No defined principles - Each project follows its own approach with no consistency
- **Level 3:** Principles documented but not enforced - Architecture standards exist but compliance is optional or inconsistent
- **Level 5:** Well-defined and enforced principles - Clear architecture principles guide all initiatives with mandatory compliance and regular audits

**What This Measures:**
- Standards definition and clarity
- Compliance enforcement
- Consistency across projects
- Audit and monitoring practices

---

### Question 5: Technology Management
**Category:** Technology Management

**Question:** How effectively do you manage your technology portfolio and application landscape?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** No portfolio management - Limited visibility into applications, technologies, or technical debt
- **Level 3:** Basic portfolio tracking - Application inventory exists but lacks integration details, lifecycle management, or rationalization processes
- **Level 5:** Comprehensive portfolio management - Complete application/technology catalog with lifecycle management, TCO analysis, and active rationalization initiatives

**What This Measures:**
- Application portfolio visibility
- Technology lifecycle management
- Technical debt awareness
- Rationalization and optimization efforts

---

### Question 6: Integration & Interoperability
**Category:** Integration & Interoperability

**Question:** What is the maturity level of your integration architecture and data management?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** Point-to-point integrations - No integration strategy, custom connections, data silos across systems
- **Level 3:** Emerging integration platform - Some standard integration patterns and tools in use but not enterprise-wide
- **Level 5:** Enterprise integration platform - Standardized integration architecture (API/ESB), data governance, and master data management practices

**What This Measures:**
- Integration architecture maturity
- Data governance and quality
- API management practices
- Master data management

---

### Question 7: Cloud & Modernization
**Category:** Cloud & Modernization

**Question:** How advanced is your cloud adoption and application modernization strategy?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** On-premise legacy systems - No cloud strategy, applications remain on traditional infrastructure
- **Level 3:** Initial cloud adoption - Some workloads migrated (lift-and-shift) with basic cloud services utilization
- **Level 5:** Cloud-native architecture - Strategic cloud-first approach with containerization, microservices, and continuous modernization roadmap

**What This Measures:**
- Cloud adoption maturity
- Modernization strategy and execution
- Cloud-native capabilities
- Technology currency

---

### Question 8: Security & Compliance
**Category:** Security & Compliance

**Question:** How mature is your enterprise security architecture and compliance framework?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** Reactive security - Security addressed on ad-hoc basis, no consistent security architecture or compliance tracking
- **Level 3:** Basic security controls - Standard security measures implemented but limited enterprise-wide security architecture or automation
- **Level 5:** Comprehensive security architecture - Zero-trust model, automated security controls, continuous compliance monitoring, and security by design principles

**What This Measures:**
- Security architecture maturity
- Compliance framework effectiveness
- Security automation
- Risk management practices

---

### Question 9: EA Capability & Skills
**Category:** EA Capability & Skills

**Question:** What is the maturity of your Enterprise Architecture team and organizational capability?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** No dedicated EA function - Architecture responsibilities distributed across IT with no formal EA practice
- **Level 3:** Emerging EA capability - Small EA team established but limited influence, tools, or organizational recognition
- **Level 5:** Mature EA organization - Well-staffed EA team with clear roles, executive sponsorship, advanced tools (modeling, repository), and recognized value across the organization

**What This Measures:**
- EA team structure and staffing
- Executive sponsorship and support
- EA tools and capabilities
- Organizational influence and value recognition

---

### Question 10: Transformation & Roadmap
**Category:** Transformation & Roadmap

**Question:** How effectively do you plan and execute EA transformation initiatives?

**Options:**
- **Level 0:** No transformation roadmap - Changes are reactive and project-driven without strategic architecture direction
- **Level 3:** Basic roadmap exists - Multi-year technology plans documented but execution is inconsistent and lacks measurable outcomes
- **Level 5:** Strategic transformation management - Clear target architecture, sequenced roadmap with business case, KPIs tracked, and regular progress reviews with stakeholders

**What This Measures:**
- Roadmap planning and execution
- Target architecture definition
- Business case development
- KPI tracking and progress monitoring

## Interpretation Guide

### Overall Maturity Levels

**Average Score 0-1.5: Initial Stage**
- EA practice is in early stages
- Heavy reliance on individual knowledge
- High risk due to lack of structure
- **Recommendation:** Establish basic EA foundations, documentation, and governance

**Average Score 1.5-3.5: Developing Stage**
- EA practices emerging but inconsistent
- Some structure and processes in place
- Pockets of excellence but not enterprise-wide
- **Recommendation:** Standardize processes, improve consistency, increase stakeholder engagement

**Average Score 3.5-5.0: Optimized Stage**
- Mature EA practice recognized across organization
- Consistent execution of EA processes
- Continuous improvement culture
- **Recommendation:** Maintain excellence, focus on innovation, mentor other organizations

### Priority Actions by Category Score

**Low Scores (0-1):**
- **Immediate Action Required**
- Allocate resources and budget
- Establish basic frameworks
- Quick wins to demonstrate value

**Medium Scores (2-3):**
- **Standardization Focus**
- Document and formalize existing practices
- Increase automation
- Expand coverage across organization

**High Scores (4-5):**
- **Optimization & Innovation**
- Maintain current practices
- Share knowledge across organization
- Explore emerging technologies and practices

## Best Practices for Taking the Assessment

### For Individual Respondents
1. **Be Honest:** Answer based on actual current state, not aspirations
2. **Consider Organization-Wide:** Think beyond your immediate team
3. **Use Evidence:** Base answers on observable practices and artifacts
4. **When Unsure:** Choose the lower option if practices are inconsistent

### For Survey Administrators
1. **Timing:** Allow 15-20 minutes for thoughtful responses
2. **Audience:** Target EA practitioners, IT leaders, and key stakeholders
3. **Sample Size:** Collect 5-10+ responses for reliable aggregate results
4. **Frequency:** Reassess quarterly or bi-annually to track progress

### For Results Analysis
1. **Look for Patterns:** Identify consistently low-scoring dimensions
2. **Compare Perspectives:** Differences between respondents indicate alignment issues
3. **Prioritize Actions:** Focus on areas with biggest business impact
4. **Track Progress:** Compare results over time to measure improvement

## Customization Guidelines

Organizations can customize this assessment by:

1. **Adding Industry-Specific Questions**
   - Financial services: Regulatory compliance depth
   - Healthcare: Interoperability and HIPAA
   - Manufacturing: OT/IT convergence

2. **Adjusting Scoring Thresholds**
   - More granular 7-point scale
   - Weighted scoring by strategic importance

3. **Adding Context Questions**
   - Organization size and complexity
   - Industry vertical
   - Geographic distribution

4. **Incorporating Qualitative Feedback**
   - Open-ended questions for each dimension
   - Success stories and challenges
   - Improvement suggestions
